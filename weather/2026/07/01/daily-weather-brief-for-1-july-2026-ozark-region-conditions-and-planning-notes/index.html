TECUMSEH, MO | The Ozark Gazette is publishing the Daily Weather Brief for 1 July 2026 for Tecumseh, Gainesville, St. Louis, Springfield, Branson, St. Charles and Fayetteville using official National Weather Service and NOAA source material.

Region at a glance

Wednesday is expected to be hot and mostly dry across the Ozark region. Forecast snapshots show mostly sunny or hazy sunshine during the day, with afternoon highs near 91°F in Fayetteville, 92°F in Springfield, 94°F in Tecumseh and Gainesville, 95°F in Branson, 97°F in St. Charles and 98°F in St. Louis. Overnight and late-evening conditions trend mostly clear to clear across the region.

The St. Louis and St. Charles areas remain under official Extreme Heat Warnings, with dangerous heat index values possible. Readers in those areas should limit strenuous outdoor activity, check on vulnerable neighbors and pets, and follow current National Weather Service guidance.

What This Means

Readers should treat this as a planning snapshot, not a replacement for official emergency instructions. The biggest concern for 1 July is heat, especially in the St. Louis and St. Charles areas where official Extreme Heat Warnings remain active.

Outdoor work, youth sports, lake travel, errands and public events should be planned around heat safety. Check current forecasts, alerts and local authorities before travel, outdoor work, school activities or public events.
