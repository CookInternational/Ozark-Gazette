News Staff

Newsroom Reporter

Beats: Weather briefs, traffic updates, sports roundups, community notices, public records and source-led daily service coverage.

News Staff represents the newsroom desk work behind many daily service updates. The newsroom reviews official weather alerts, road information, sports schedules, public records and community notices, then turns that source material into clear reader-friendly briefs for Ozark County. Sensitive items, including deaths, courts, crime and investigations, remain subject to editorial review before publication.

Michael A. Cook

Editor-in-Chief

Beats: Editorial leadership, Politics, World, Markets, public accountability, investigations and major public-affairs coverage.

A Chicagoland native, Michael A. Cook is the Editor-in-Chief of CGN News. A veteran of the Army National Guard and a Ball State University alumnus, Michael brings a grounded, real-world perspective to coverage of Politics, World, and Markets. His editorial focus centers on public accountability, institutional decision-making, economic change, and the national and global developments that shape everyday life. At CGN News, he leads with an emphasis on clear reporting, independent analysis, and coverage that connects major events to their real-world impact.

editor@cgnnews.net

Jordan Whitaker

Staff Reporter

Beats: Local news, civic affairs, business corridors, public safety, neighborhoods, Missouri and Illinois metro coverage.

Jordan Whitaker is a Staff Reporter for The Ozark Gazette, bringing local-affiliate coverage experience across the city, St. Louis County, Metro East, and the wider Missouri-Illinois region. Born in Florissant and educated at the University of Missouri–St. Louis, Jordan focuses on local government, transportation, public safety, neighborhood redevelopment, riverfront commerce, and the civic decisions that shape daily life. Jordan is known for keeping a whiteboard full of ward maps, transit notes, and weather alerts, and believes every St. Louis story eventually connects back to neighborhoods, schools, sports, or the Mississippi River.

tips@cgnnews.net

Elise Navarro

Meteorologist

Beats: Weather, severe storms, river flooding, heat, winter weather, travel impacts and emergency preparedness.

Elise Navarro is the Meteorologist for CGN St. Louis, covering severe thunderstorms, tornado risk, river flooding, heat, winter systems, and daily forecast impacts across Missouri and Illinois. Born in Belleville and educated at Saint Louis University, Elise became interested in weather after childhood spring storms turned family plans into radar-watching sessions. Her reporting translates watches, warnings, river gauges, and forecast uncertainty into practical guidance for commuters, schools, sports fans, and families. Elise keeps a small collection of vintage weather radios and is the person most likely to explain a cold front with a napkin sketch.

tips@cgnnews.net

Marcus Bell

Sports Desk

Beats: Cardinals, Blues, City SC, Battlehawks, SLU, Mizzou, Illinois, SIUE, Lindenwood and local college sports.

Marcus Bell runs the CGN St. Louis Sports Desk, following professional, college, and community sports across one of America’s most loyal sports cities. Born in South City and educated at Webster University, Marcus covers the Cardinals, Blues, St. Louis City SC, Battlehawks, SLU, Mizzou, Illinois programs, SIUE, Lindenwood, and the high school traditions that fill Friday nights. He approaches sports as civic culture: stadiums, neighborhoods, rivalries, alumni networks, and family calendars all matter. Marcus collects old scorecards, refuses to leave a game early, and can usually identify a ballpark by the press-box view.
