INDIANAPOLIS | Indiana business records, court records, company websites, source communications, training materials and public-facing social media materials reviewed by CGN News raise new questions about the people behind Illuminaughty, a Carmel-linked adult toy and OnlyFans model-management brand that publicly describes itself as female-owned and veteran-owned.

The records reviewed by CGN connect Illuminaughty-branded entities to James Rothschild and Randall James Rothschild, names associated with multiple Indiana business filings using Carmel, Fishers and Zionsville addresses. CGN News also reviewed public LinkedIn materials showing 2LT Emily J. Riggle, an Indiana Army National Guard officer, listing Rothschild Group as her employer and “Administrative & Operations Officer” as her job title.

Internal Guard source says complaint was received

An internal Indiana National Guard source told CGN News that the Guard received a complaint concerning Riggle’s adult-business connections and conducted an investigation.

That account places the matter inside the Guard’s internal review process. It also raises a clear public-interest question: whether a commissioned officer’s outside business ties involving adult retail, adult model management or OnlyFans-related marketing were disclosed, reviewed or cleared through appropriate military channels.

Department of Defense ethics guidance says outside activities are generally permitted, but personnel may not have outside employment or be involved in outside activity that conflicts with official duties. Federal ethics rules also say employees must comply with restrictions on conflicting outside employment and, where applicable, agency-specific prior-approval requirements. Department of Defense Standards of Conduct Office Federal outside-employment ethics rules

The relevant public question is whether the outside business activity was disclosed, whether it was reviewed, and whether the Guard considered the public-facing “Female Owned” and “Veteran Owned” language used by Illuminaughty.

CGN News is seeking a formal response from the Indiana National Guard.

Why it matters

Military officers hold positions of public trust. Many service members lawfully operate businesses, work second jobs, invest in companies or build private ventures. Outside employment is not automatically improper.

But outside business interests can become a public concern when they involve sensitive industries, public-facing claims of veteran ownership, anonymous complaints, adult-content management systems or questions about whether official policies were followed.

Adult toy sales and adult creator-management services are legal commercial sectors. The accountability question is whether the public record matches the private business structure, whether required disclosures were made, and whether the Guard’s review process addressed the questions raised by the complaint.

For now, CGN’s review shows a records-backed connection among Illuminaughty-branded entities, James Rothschild / Randall James Rothschild, the 550 Congressional Blvd. Carmel address, public-facing adult retail and creator-management materials, Riggle’s Rothschild Group employment listing, source communications involving Riggle, Banx-linked model-management training materials and an internal Guard source who says a complaint was received and investigated.
